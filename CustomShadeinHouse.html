why choose in-house shading?

the problem and the solution

challenge - solution - result

In highly aesthetic cases, shade is not just color. It is value, translucency, texture, and light behavior. Our in-house model captures these details in one controlled visit.

higher match accuracy Live patient shading captures details shade tabs cannot.
lower adjustment risk Accurate mapping minimizes chairside corrections and remakes.
stronger patient trust Natural blending improves confidence in final esthetic outcomes.
01

the challenge

Standard guides like VITA are 2D references for a 3D, translucent structure. They often miss micro-character, cervical warmth, and incisal blue-grey effects.

02

the solution

With in-house visits, our ceramists observe the live tooth and map high-value zones, texture, and reflection in a controlled environment.

03

the result

Drastically fewer remakes and a reliable shade-match route for single-central and high-demand cosmetic restorations.

at the lab

where the live tooth meets the restoration

In-house shading is not a photo sent across town - it is observation in a calibrated space. We match translucency, surface luster, and cervical warmth while you and your patient are still in the conversation.

That context carries straight into the next step: our short, structured protocol you'll see in the Zann Standard - so expectations stay aligned from consult to ceramist bench.

the zann standard

our 20-minute in-house shade process

20 min

A structured four-step protocol removes visual bias and turns what we see at the chair into production-ready ceramic detail - fast, calm, and repeatable.

5500K daylight polarized macro patient intent live stain option
1

controlled environment

We use 5500K color-corrected lighting (Northern Daylight standard) to remove yellow/blue perception shifts common in many clinics.

2

digital mapping

Polarized macro photography helps us read enamel/dentin interaction and internal optical behavior beyond simple A1/B2 coding.

3

characterization interview

We align with patient preference: youthful high-translucency aesthetics or mature, textured natural appearance.

4

real-time custom stain (optional)

If restoration is already in-lab, we can provide complimentary custom stain and glaze while the patient waits.

for doctors

artisanal craftsmanship meets CAD/CAM precision

Your veneers and implant crowns are not only milled - they are hand-finished for life-like optical behavior. Our custom shade data is integrated directly into the final layering strategy.

  • High-translucency zirconia protocols for controlled strength and esthetics.
  • Hand-layered e.max finishing for depth, vitality, and natural transitions.
  • Surface texture and gloss tuning based on live characterization notes.

Patient Etiquette Guide

Simple Pro-Tips for Best Shade Accuracy

Neutral Palette

Please wear neutral clothing (grey, white, beige). Bright colors can reflect onto teeth and affect color perception.

No Makeup

We recommend removing lipstick and heavy foundation. Lip and skin tones influence visual shade interpretation.

Morning Appointments

Whenever possible, morning sessions are ideal when visual fatigue is lower and daylight balance is most reliable.
