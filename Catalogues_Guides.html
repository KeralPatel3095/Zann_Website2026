chairside alignment

clinical protocol manuals

Shared language on reduction and cementation reduces remakes and protects your warranty path. These are the numbers we design to.

preparation requirements

Ideal reduction keeps ceramic in its strength window without over-cutting tooth structure. As a working guide: plan 1.0 - 1.5 mm of clearance for e.max crowns where occlusal and axial walls allow full contour; for veneers, target roughly 0.5 - 0.7 mm labial reduction with defined incisal transitions so we can preserve enamel for bonding.

cementation & bonding

Lithium disilicate benefits from adhesive resin protocols that maximize enamel and dentin coupling - ask for our latest IFU pairing for your cement brand.

Zirconia requires surface conditioning and primers matched to the zirconia generation you receive; we outline chairside steps so retention is predictable under functional load.
